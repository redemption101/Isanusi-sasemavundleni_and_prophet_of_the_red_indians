How does African cosmology define the cycle of life and reincarnation?

bloodline and the earth: 
● Birth and Spiritual Anchoring: When a child is born, the ritual of imbeloko is

● The Transition of Aging: African culture recognises a mirror relationship between youth 
and old age; as people grow old, they become like babies again, needing the same care

● Death and the Ancestral Path: At death, the soul does not go to a celestial heaven but

returned to finish her work,.

● The Frontend (Isthunwa): A female spirit guide (such as a grandmother) walks ahead to

7. War and Armed Conflict 
Ongoing conflicts in regions like Ukraine, Gaza, Sudan, and Yemen demonstrate the severe 
human and economic toll of international hostilities. These wars cause direct loss of life, disrupt 
global supply chains, and create massive humanitarian crises. The threat of nuclear weapons 
expansion remains a critical global concern. 
Impediments to Solving Mega-Challenges 
Despite the urgent need for global solutions, several factors hinder international collaboration: 
● Competing National Interests: Countries often prioritize short-term domestic needs 
over long-term global priorities. 
● Political and Ideological Divides: Different views on governance and human rights 
engender distrust among nations. 
● Bureaucracy: Large multinational organizations like the UN or WHO can be slow to 
respond during crises. 
● Internal Polarization: Political divisions within countries, such as in the United States, 
can prevent a national consensus on international roles. 
In response to these challenges, frameworks like the "Ten-C" spectrum of international 
relations emphasize moving from negative states like conflict and competition toward positive 
states such as coordination, cooperation, and collaboration.
